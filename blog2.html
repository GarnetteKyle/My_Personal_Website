The Rat Race: Everyone Is Running, But Where?


Posted by: Garnette Kyle Valenzuela
January 20, 2026
Designed by Freepik

Lately, I have been contemplating my future. I find myself constantly overthinking what I will become, and the questions run in my head like a while loop that has not met its condition. Will I be able to get a good job? What if I fail? What if I get left behind? What if I wasn’t able to achieve the life I want? What if I become no one? I have fallen into the trap of treating life like a high stakes competition where a single mistake feels like a disqualification. My life just started, but I already feel desperate, wanting to achieve something that is quite impossible to achieve in a short span of time. I am too focused worrying about the future where I forgot to live in the present.

Are we working hard to live, or are we living to work?

But recently, something snapped in my head. I asked myself, Why am I doing this? I realized I was trying to build the roof of a house before I had even poured the concrete for the foundation. I am obsessed with what I should be doing in the next few years, but I haven’t even built my foundation yet. I have been getting blinded by the lavish and luxurious lifestyles I see on social media every single day.

It is a strange kind of torture to scroll through a feed and see people my age, or even younger, who seem to have it all figured out. I see the travel, the expensive cars, and the aesthetic offices, and suddenly, my own room feels too small. My own progress feels like zero. It makes me feel like I’m standing still while the rest of the world is running toward a finish line I can't even see.

Designed by Freepik

This rat race isn't actually about moving forward. It is about the paralyzing fear of being seen as less than others. I realized I’ve been running a marathon on a treadmill. I am exhausted, sweaty, and breathless, yet I am staying in the exact same place because my progress was purely mental. I’ve been so consumed by the idea of a lavish lifestyle that I’ve started to view my current reality as a failure rather than a beginning. By obsessing over a version of myself that doesn't exist yet, I was effectively skipping the very chapters of my life that are meant to define who I am. Those chapters include the struggle, the confusion, and the small, boring steps of learning.

We are taught that success is a sprint, but I’m realizing that a foundation built on speed rather than depth is destined to crumble. You cannot rush the growth of a tree, and you certainly cannot rush the growth of a human life. The more I look at those filtered screens, the more I lose sight of the unfiltered potential of my own hands. I’ve been so busy looking at where everyone else is running that I haven't noticed I’m already on my own path. It just doesn't look like theirs, and that has to be okay.

If success were invisible, would you still chase it the same way?

I am learning that you cannot rush a foundation. If I build my life on the speed of achievement rather than the depth of learning, the structure will eventually fall apart. I’m choosing to break the loop of overthinking. Instead of fearing I’ll become no one, I am focusing on becoming myself. The rat race only has power if you believe the finish line is real. But the truth is that the finish line keeps moving every time you get close to it.

I’m stepping off the track to realize that life isn't a race to be won, but a story to be lived. I don't want to look back in ten years and realize I was so busy worrying about the future that I never actually experienced being young. The future will arrive in its own time. For now, I am finally content to just be here, building my foundation one brick at a time and learning to breathe again.

You don’t lose the rat race by slowing down, you lose it by never questioning it.
