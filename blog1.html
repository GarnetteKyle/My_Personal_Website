Artificial Intelligence: The Take Over, Will Programmers Survive?


Posted by: Garnette Kyle Valenzuela
January 15, 2026
Designed by Freepik

I am certain that when you’re scrolling through social media (Tiktok,Facebook,YouTube), there are instances that you’ve encountered a post in your feed that says Artificial Intelligence (AI) would take over programmers jobs in the near future. Many are worried, and afraid, contemplating whether they continue the path to this career or not.

And I am one of them.
As someone who is still learning and building my skills in programming, seeing these posts sometimes makes me doubt myself and wonder if the effort I’m putting in will still matter in the future.


It is true that AI did manage to help us on a day to day basis. Students, Teachers, Workers, and even elderly. Like students using Generative AI to help them study. Can’t understand the formula of a specific math formula? AI. Need to find a definition of a specific word? AI. Need a guide to do a specific task? AI.

Designed by Freepik

But what seems to appear as an ordinary tool, can actually replace you. With one prompt, it can generate a graphic design,audio, and program codes which ironically created them in the first place.

Recently, there are a lot of companies laying off their employees, even programmers. What replace them? Still humans but it is a human who knows AI, how it works, and how to build it. They can do as much as one department can do. What does it mean? It means less employees then less payroll, less employment then less job opportunities.

As a college student, it’s making me worry. It is not just about logical thinking and programming anymore. I am just about to graduate and I’m already about to compete with hundreds of thousands of people who’s also seeking employment and have better skills than me.

That means that I need to have the knowledge and skills of professionals as soon as I graduate. But it is what it is, we can’t and we can never stop evolution. The only thing we can do is to adapt in order to survive. Utilize AI itself to gain knowledge and skills.

So will programmers survive? Yes, for people who see AI as a tool, not god.
