Childhood Teenage College

A Life in Progress

Childhood Years

Hi, my name is Garnette Kyle Valenzuela, I was born and raised in Tondo, Manila, and I can’t wait to leave this place.1 My friends always tease me because of my name.2 Some say I was a basketball player because my name is similar to Kevin Garnett.3 And some say my name is derived from Garnet Stone.4 But my name is just literally a logical mix of my parents’ names.5 When I was a kid, I always put myself in trouble.6 There was an accident where I was running and tripped, and my left forearm got sliced by an iron sheet.7 Another time, I was playing with my classmates and tripped again, and my tooth broke.8 I also remember the day my friend almost hit my eye with a pencil.9 I can say that I had a happy childhood.10 But of course, there are always two sides of the coin.11 I also experienced bullying because I was overweight back then.12 I have friends, but there are very few classmates who, for some reason, love bullying.13 I always got involved in fights because of it.14 But I had already built the character of a nice, kind, quiet, and gentle boy in my teachers’ and classmates’ minds, that’s why I was never scolded by our teachers or had a bad record in the Guidance Office.15 I hope they’re doing fine.16 We were kids back then, we make mistakes and still have a lot of lessons to learn.17 Looking back, those early scrapes taught me resilience.18 I learned that pain is temporary but memories last.19 This foundation shaped who I am today.20

Teenage Years

I studied at Jose Abad Santos High School in Binondo, Manila.1 When I was in 7th grade, I had a lot of friends.2 We always played mobile games every vacant time, walking back home together after class.3 But in 8th grade, I felt like I didn’t belong.4 I only had two friends, while the whole class was always together and getting along with each other.5 That’s why at that time, all I did was play online games.6 The school year was still ongoing when COVID came.7 Almost my whole junior high school was conducted online.8 I didn’t know what to feel at that time, whether to be happy to avoid socializing with people or sad because I might have missed one of the happiest stages of my life.9 After 10th grade, everything started to go back to normal.10 Since I spent the whole time playing online games, I took ICT–Computer Programming as my senior high school strand and track.11 I told myself that I cannot live isolating myself; that’s why I started socializing and making friends with everyone, and it went well.12 I met a lot of friends.13 I really loved my senior high school days as they were one of the happiest moments of my life.14 But it is also one of the stages of my life that I want to forget.15 Things turned bitter.16 After graduation, I cut ties.17 I just hope that in college, I will build something again and move forward in life.18 I want to leave those bitter memories behind.19 I am ready for a new chapter.20

College Years

College came, and I still can’t believe that I will be studying at the University of the East under the BSCS program.1 I was considering studying at cheap private colleges, but my aunts insisted that I study here instead.2 I did try to apply to state universities, but I was only able to take the entrance exam at PLM.3 I still remember putting a sad emoticon on the back of my scratch paper because I knew deep inside that I had flunked the exam.4 I don’t even know why I felt sad, knowing that I hadn’t even spent a single day reviewing.5 Then I received an email from PLM, and no, it was not a plot twist showing that I passed.6 It was just to show me that I really failed the exam.7 Now, let's focus on the actual college experience.8 On my first day of class, I entered the room knowing I was early, but for some reason, there was a professor sitting at the front.9 I asked if I was in the correct section, and I was.10 Then, our professor realized that we were not supposed to meet that day, it was supposed to be the next day.11 It was so funny that it happened during the first day that I remember it up to this day.12 Moving forward, I met a lot of friends, especially during my NSTP-ROTC days.13 I experienced many new things I had never tried before, traveling to far places, eating outside, and just enjoying life.14 College is tiring, both physically and mentally.15 To be honest, I sometimes feel like I’m getting left behind, doubting if I really belong in this program.16 But I know deep inside that it is part of the process, and I won’t let it consume me or bring me down.17 Yes, college is tiring, but that is also where you feel alive.18 I still make mistakes, and sometimes I get frustrated, but I try to laugh at myself.19 I always think of the life I want, so I will try my best and keep moving forward.20
