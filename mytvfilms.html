My Favorite TV Shows

Genre: Sci‑Fi / Drama

The 100

Directors: Jason Rothenberg, Ed Fraiman, Dean White
Main Cast: Eliza Taylor, Bob Morley, Marie Avgeropoulos
Released: 2014–2020
Rating: 7.6/10 IMDb
A gripping post-apocalyptic sci-fi drama that begins a century after a nuclear apocalypse devastates Earth. The story follows 100 juvenile delinquents sent from a space station to determine if Earth is habitable. They must navigate deadly threats, form alliances, and confront moral dilemmas while rebuilding society. Themes of leadership, survival, and ethical ambiguity run throughout the series.

Genre: K‑Drama / Romance / Fantasy

Extraordinary You

Director: Kim Sang‑hyeop
Main Cast: Kim Hye‑yoon, Ro Woon
Released: 2019
Rating: 8.2/10 IMDb
A unique K-drama where a high school girl discovers she is a background character in a comic book and struggles to take control of her destiny. As she navigates relationships, school life, and the boundaries between fiction and reality, she challenges fate, explores self-discovery, and experiences love, friendship, and personal growth in a fantastical yet relatable setting.

Genre: Horror / Drama / Thriller

The Walking Dead

Directors: Frank Darabont, Greg Nicotero, Gwyneth Horder‑Payton, Ernest Dickerson, Johan Renck
Main Cast: Andrew Lincoln, Norman Reedus, Melissa McBride
Released: 2010–2022
Rating: 8.2/10 IMDb
A tense and emotional post-apocalyptic series that follows survivors of a global zombie outbreak. Beyond the relentless threat of the undead, the survivors face human adversaries, ethical dilemmas, and the collapse of societal structures. The show explores themes of morality, leadership, community, and resilience in a world where survival often comes at a heavy cost.

Genre: Crime / Drama / Thriller

Narcos (Pablo Escobar)

Directors: Andrés Baiz, Josef Kubota Wladyka, Fernando Coimbra
Main Cast: Wagner Moura, Pedro Pascal
Released: 2015–2017
Rating: 8.8/10 IMDb
A gripping crime drama chronicling the rise and fall of Colombian drug lord Pablo Escobar, whose cartel dominated the global cocaine trade. Told from both law enforcement and Escobar’s perspective, the series delves into corruption, violence, political intrigue, and the personal consequences of power. It highlights the complexity of human ambition and the impact of the drug trade on society.
